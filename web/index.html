Let's build it!

🔥
Before you startA couple of steps use a hot soldering iron. Work on a heat-safe surface, keep the tip away from your fingers, and let it cool on its stand. New to soldering? There are great beginner guides on the project page.
1

Prep the pin headers

Get your little rows of pins ready so everything can plug in. Line them up and pop them into place — like sorting your LEGO before a build.

Headers, ready to go
💡
TipPush the pins into a breadboard first to hold them straight while you solder — they'll come out perfectly aligned.
2

Put it all together

Snap the brain, the screen, and the keys onto the board. Each part has one spot and only fits the right way — take your time and press gently.

Coming together! 🧩
3

Give it a voice (solder the speaker)

Connect the speaker and its wires. This is what lets your keyboard actually make sound — no speaker, no music!

Wiring up the sound
🔴
Super important!Don't forget to short the J4 jumper — bridge the two little J4 pads together. Miss this and your kit stays silent. See the close-up below 👇
👆 Short THIS one — J4
4

Power up!

Two ways to switch it on: plug in a USB cable and play right away, or add a small rechargeable battery to jam anywhere — no wires!

Go wireless with a battery 🔋
🔋
Battery safetyUse a single-cell (1S) 3.3–4.2 V 18650 cell. Double-check the + and − are the right way round before powering on, and never bend or puncture the cell.
5

Make it sing! (add the software)

Your keyboard's brain needs its "song instructions." Grab the ready-made software from the project page and load it on — then touch a key and listen! 🎶

How does it work?

✋

It feels your finger

The keys sense the tiny electric charge in your body — so a gentle touch is all it takes. No buttons to push!

🧠

A tiny brain

The ESP32 chip listens to the keys and decides which note to play, thousands of times every second.

🎻

Maths that sounds like strings

It doesn't play recordings — it invents a plucked-string sound from scratch using clever maths. That's why it sounds like a harp!

🔧

Want to tinker?

This kit is a playground for makers — you can absolutely code it yourself and wire in your own inputs.

💻

Code it yourself

The firmware is fully open source. Change the notes, the scale, even how it makes sound — reprogram it into your very own instrument.

🗺️

Full pin maps

Every pin and wiring detail is documented on GitHub, so you know exactly what connects where.

🔌

Bring your own inputs

The kit breaks out an input connector, so you can plug in your own array of buttons, switches or sensors for a wider, wilder keyboard — no touch pads required.

🍌
